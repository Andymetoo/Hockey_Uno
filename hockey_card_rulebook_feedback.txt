HOCKEY CARD PROTOTYPE
RULEBOOK FOR FEEDBACK

This document explains the current rules of the Hockey Card Prototype as implemented in the browser build. It is written for feedback and playtest discussion, not as final published packaging copy.

1. GAME OVERVIEW

This is a head-to-head card game about gaining possession, advancing the puck up the ice, creating shots, and scoring goals.

Each side has a hand of cards. A card has a suit and a value.

The main loop is:

1. A faceoff determines who starts with possession.
2. The attacker begins an exchange by playing cards that match the active card.
3. The defender answers with matching cards of their own.
4. The exchange ends when a player stops, runs out of legal plays, or wins/loses the comparison.
5. The puck either advances, stays put, or changes possession.
6. If an attacker empties their hand in a shot zone, a shot occurs.
7. A goal usually leads to a center-ice faceoff (except sudden-death overtime, where the goal ends the game). A save leads to a faceoff in the shot area.

The prototype now tracks score across regulation and overtime.

- The game is played over 3 regulation periods.
- If one side leads after Period 3 ends, that side wins the game.
- If the score is tied after Period 3, the game goes to OT1, then OT2, and so on.
- Overtime is sudden death: the next goal wins.

2. COMPONENTS

Cards

- Each card has a suit and a value.
- By default, the prototype uses 4 suits: Pace, Finesse, Grit, and Power.
- Vision can be added in expanded suit-count setups.
- Wild cards are optional. In the current default setup, there are no wild cards.

Deck construction

- The deck contains two copies of every active suit/value combination.
- Default setup: 4 suits, values 1 through 8, 5-card hands, 0 wild cards.

Hands

- Each player normally plays from a 5-card hand in the default setup.
- Hands are refilled in certain state changes described later in this document.

Zones

The rink is represented by six zones:

1. Human Goal Zone
2. Human Defensive Zone
3. Neutral Zone A
4. Neutral Zone B
5. CPU Defensive Zone
6. CPU Goal Zone

Center ice is treated as the space between the two neutral zones. End-zone faceoffs are also represented between paired zones.

3. OBJECTIVE

Your short-term goal is to win exchanges and move the puck toward the opponent's end.

Your scoring goal is to empty your hand in a shot zone, force a save challenge, and beat that challenge to score.

3.1 PERIODS AND CLOCK

The prototype uses a deck-based game clock instead of tracking real-time seconds.

- Each period starts at 20:00.
- The default pacing is 4 decks per period, but this is configurable in debug setup.
- Time is linked to deck progression: each deck in a period is worth an equal slice of the 20:00 period clock.
- The clock updates as cards are drawn during live play, rather than only jumping at reshuffle moments.
- Setup refills for period-opening faceoffs do not consume clock.
- If the period clock reaches 0:00 when a deck cycle completes, the period ends immediately, even if it happens in the middle of a play sequence.
- When a period ends, play stops and the next period begins with a center-ice faceoff.
- There is no side switch between periods in the current prototype.

4. TURN STRUCTURE

Every live sequence has an attacker and a defender.

- The attacker is the player with possession.
- The defender is the player trying to answer the attack.
- The current turn always belongs to one player in one role.

An exchange has two sides:

1. Attack phase: the attacker plays one or more legal cards.
2. Defense phase: the defender tries to answer and beat or hold the exchange.

The exchange is measured by card count:

- Attacker chain count = how many cards the attacker played in that exchange.
- Defender chain count = how many cards the defender played in that exchange.

Current core states

- Standard Faceoff: a normal restart after game start, goals, saves, and period starts.
- Loose Puck Faceoff: a special in-place restart created by a 0-0 exchange when Momentum System is ON.
- Icing Faceoff: a special defensive-zone restart created by the Ice Puck / Icing action.
- Live Exchange: the normal attack phase followed by the defense phase.
- Stabilize Attempt: a one-card emergency answer the attacker gets when the defender leads the exchange by exactly 1.
- Save Chain: the defender's response state after a shot is created.
- Period Break: live play stops, the current sequence is cleared, and the next period begins with a center-ice faceoff.
- Game Over: the match has ended and no further play is resolved.

5. THE ACTIVE CARD AND MATCHING RULE

The top card of the pile is the active card.

To play a legal card, the card must match the active card by at least one of these:

- Same suit
- Same value
- Wild interaction

If there is no active card, any card may start the pile.

Wild cards

- A wild card acts as a bridge and is always legal against the active card.
- If either the played card or the active card is wild, the match is treated as a legal wild bridge.

6. RIVER RULE / MATCH LOCKING

Default rule

- In the default prototype setting, value and suit matching may alternate freely.
- In that mode, a legal play simply has to match suit, value, or both.

Optional single-cross mode

- The prototype includes an optional "Cross River Once" mode.
- In that mode, once a run becomes suit-matching or value-matching, it locks to that type.
- A player may switch once from suit to value, or from value to suit, during that run.
- After that single switch, the run stays locked.
- A card that matches both suit and value does not force the run to lock either way if no lock exists yet.

Important prototype note

- River tracking is handled per active run, not globally for the whole match.
- Each player gets their own run state when their side of the exchange begins.

7. FACEOFFS

The game begins with a faceoff at center ice. Other standard faceoffs happen after goals, saves, and period starts. Icing creates a special Icing Faceoff in the icing side's defensive end.

Standard faceoff

- The Human chooses one card.
- The CPU reveals one card.
- The winner becomes the attacker.
- The winner's card becomes the active card on the pile.
- The puck is placed in the winner's resulting faceoff zone.

Icing Faceoff

- The player who iced must reveal and commit a faceoff card first.
- The opponent then chooses a faceoff card after seeing that reveal.
- The same normal faceoff suit/value rules still decide the winner.
- The winner becomes the attacker, the winner's card becomes the active card, and the puck goes to the normal resulting faceoff zone.

Faceoff suit cycle

The suit priority uses a clockwise cycle based on the active suit set.

Default 4-suit cycle:

Grit > Finesse > Pace > Power > Grit

Faceoff resolution rules

- If both cards share a suit, the higher value wins.
- If an exact same-suit tie occurs, the winner is chosen randomly.
- If wild cards are involved in a faceoff, the comparison falls back to value.
- In odd-suit loops, each suit beats the next half of the cycle clockwise.
- In 2-suit setups, different suits fall back entirely to value.
- In even loops larger than 2 suits, a suit beats its immediate clockwise neighbor, while opposite suits fall back to value.
- Exact fallback ties are resolved randomly.

After the faceoff resolves, normal attack play begins.

Current prototype note

- Any faceoff clears both sides' Momentum.
- Standard faceoffs refill hands before the faceoff begins.
- Icing Faceoffs also refill both hands before the faceoff begins, then force the icing side to reveal first.
- Loose Puck faceoffs do not use that full pre-faceoff refill step; they resolve in the current zone and only the winner may refill afterward if that player spent the last card.
- The faceoff cards themselves are spent cards and do not automatically refill after the faceoff resolves.
- Only a player who somehow reaches an empty hand after the faceoff draws a fresh hand.

8. EXCHANGES

8.1 Attack phase

The attacker starts by playing a legal card.

- If there is no active card, any card can start the pile.
- Otherwise, the attacker must match the active card under the current matching rule.

The attacker may continue the chain as long as legal plays remain.

The Human attacker can stage multiple cards on a normal turn, then choose Submit to commit the turn or Undo to take back the last staged card.

8.2 Ending the attack phase

The attack phase ends when one of these happens:

- The attacker chooses End Chain or Submit.
- The attacker has no further legal continuation and the turn resolves.
- The attacker empties their hand.

When the attacker ends the chain without emptying the hand, the defender gets a chance to answer.

8.3 Defense phase

The defender tries to answer the attack with legal matching cards.

- The defender may continue chaining as long as legal answers remain.
- If the defender has already exceeded the attacker's count, the defender may end the chain and take possession.
- If the defender does not exceed the attacker, the attacker holds the exchange.

The Human defender also uses Undo and Submit on normal turns.

9. HOW EXCHANGES ARE WON

When the exchange resolves, compare card counts:

- If defender chain count is greater than attacker chain count by 2 or more, the defender wins the exchange and takes possession immediately.
- If defender chain count is greater than attacker chain count by exactly 1, the attacker gets one immediate Stabilize attempt.
- During a Stabilize attempt, the attacker may play 1 legal card. If no legal card is available, the attacker draws 1 card; if that card is legal, it is played immediately.
- If the attacker completes the Stabilize attempt, the turnover is denied, the attacker keeps possession, and there is no zone advance from that exchange.
- If the attacker cannot complete the Stabilize attempt, the turnover stands and the defender takes possession.
- Otherwise, the attacker holds or wins the exchange.

Zone advancement rule

- The attacker advances one zone only if the attacker played more cards than the defender.
- With Momentum System ON, a 0-0 exchange triggers an immediate Loose Puck faceoff in the current zone (no automatic zone advance).
- With Momentum System OFF, a 0-0 exchange still counts as forward momentum and advances the attacker.
- Non-zero ties do not advance the puck.

This means:

- Momentum ON: 0-0 creates a Loose Puck faceoff in place
- Momentum OFF: 0-0 advances
- 1-1 does not advance
- 2-1 advances
- 2-2 does not advance

If the defender wins the exchange, possession changes and the new attacker begins from the current zone.

10. DRAWING / CANNOT PLAY

The prototype includes a draw/cannot-play action.

When a player cannot legally act in the appropriate moment, they draw one card from the deck.

After drawing:

- If the drawn card is immediately playable for the CPU, the CPU may play it at once.
- If the Human draws a playable card, the Human still chooses manually whether to use it.
- If the drawn card still does not help, the turn resolves according to role.

Role-based result after an unsuccessful draw

- Attacker: the attack chain ends and passes to defense.
- Defender: the attacker wins the exchange.

Prototype-specific note

- In the current prototype flow, drawing is mainly used before a live chain has been established on that turn.
- Once a Human has already played into a chain, the intended resolution is usually to continue, End Chain, Undo, or Submit rather than draw.
- Hidden or shown action buttons follow the same legality rule: if an option is not currently legal in that state, it is not offered.

Practical order when a player appears stuck

- First, check for any legal card play.
- If the player is the attacker at the start of a new attack, check any currently legal Momentum actions.
- If Ice Puck / Icing is available, it is the defensive-zone emergency reset before surrendering the exchange.
- If Dump Hand is available, it is the offensive-zone field-position reset before surrendering the exchange.
- If no legal play or legal special action exists, use Draw / Cannot Play and resolve the turn normally.

If the deck is empty, the draw attempt fails and the same role-based resolution occurs.

11. SHOTS

A shot occurs when the attacker empties their hand while in a shot zone.

Once a shot begins, the game enters a shot sequence.

- During the shot sequence, neither player refills their hand until the full result is resolved.
- The shot sequence ends only on a goal, a frozen puck, or a defensive recovery.

Shot zones

- Far shot zone: the attacker's offensive zone
- Close shot zone: the opponent's goal zone

Shot value

- Far shot: shot value equals the played card's value.
- Close shot: shot value equals played card value + 1.

Examples

- A 2 in close range becomes a shot value of 3.
- A 6 in close range becomes a shot value of 7.

12. SAVES AND REBOUNDS

The defender answers a shot by completing a save chain.

Required save length by shot value

- Save bands scale with the current max card value.
- Let the save band size be the max card value divided by 3, rounded up.
- Shot value 1 through band size: 1 save card
- Shot value band size + 1 through band size x 2: 2 save cards
- Shot value above that: 3 save cards
- Example: with max card value 7, shot values 1-3 need 1 save card, 4-6 need 2 save cards, and 7+ need 3 save cards.

Each save card must legally continue from the current active card under the same matching system.

Normal save outcomes

- If the defender completes the full chain, the result is a save and frozen puck.
- If the defender comes up exactly 1 card short, and the shot required 2 or more save cards, a rebound occurs.
- If the defender comes up 2 or more cards short, the shot becomes a goal.
- If the shot required only 1 save card and the defender produces 0, that is a goal, not a rebound.

Rebound sequence

- On a rebound, the shooter reveals the top card of the deck.
- If that revealed card legally matches the current active card, it is played immediately as a rebound shot.
- Rebound reveals reset any prior Cross River Once state; the revealed card only has to match the current active card from scratch.
- The rebound shot's value is the revealed card's printed value.
- If the revealed card does not legally match, the defender recovers possession in their own goal zone.
- On that failed rebound reveal, the shooter keeps the revealed card, then both players refill normally after the recovery.

Rebound save

- A rebound shot requires exactly 1 legal save card.
- If the defender has no legal rebound save in hand, the defender may reveal 1 desperation save card from the top of the deck.
- If no legal save card is produced, the result is a goal.
- If the defender's rebound save value is greater than or equal to the rebound shot value, the puck is frozen.
- If the defender's rebound save value is lower than the rebound shot value, another rebound occurs.

Shot-sequence outcomes

- Frozen puck causes a faceoff in the shot area.
- Goal scores immediately and then follows normal goal reset rules.
- Defensive recovery ends the shot sequence, gives possession to the defender in their own goal zone, and then hands refill before normal play resumes.

Prototype interface note

- Human save chains and Human rebound saves are selected manually and require Submit to confirm.
- The normal multi-card staging system applies to standard attack/defense turns; shot-sequence saves use their own one-card Submit flow.

13. GOALS AND SCORING

- Each successful shot that is not fully saved scores 1 goal.
- Human goals increase Human score by 1.
- CPU goals increase CPU score by 1.
- A fully frozen shot sequence does not score; it resets play with a faceoff in the shot area.
- A defensive recovery does not score; it gives the defending side possession in its own goal zone.
- In regulation, after a goal, play resets to a center-ice faceoff.
- In sudden-death overtime, a goal ends the game immediately.

14. EMPTY-HAND SPECIAL CASES

14.1 Attacker empties hand outside a shot zone

If the attacker empties the hand outside a shot zone:

- Hands are refilled.
- The attacker wins the exchange.
- The normal exchange resolution then applies.

14.2 Attacker empties hand in a shot zone

If the attacker empties the hand in a shot zone:

- A shot is created immediately instead of resolving as a standard exchange.

14.3 Defender empties hand on defense

If the defender empties the hand while defending, a special turnover occurs.

The special turnover reward is:

- The defender immediately becomes the attacker.
- If the turnover happens in a shot zone for the new attacker, the new attacker gets an immediate shot from the current zone using the last played card.
- Otherwise, the puck auto-advances one zone, then hands refill and the new attack begins.

15. PUCK MOVEMENT

Human attacks toward the CPU end.
CPU attacks toward the Human end.

Movement is mirrored by side:

- From center ice, Human advances into Neutral Zone B and CPU advances into Neutral Zone A.
- Further exchange wins move the puck one zone deeper toward the opponent's goal.
- If already at the deepest zone, the puck stays there.

16. HUMAN TURN CONTROLS IN THE PROTOTYPE

Normal Human attack/defense turns support staged play.

Undo

- Removes the most recently staged Human card.
- Restores the prior hand, pile state, active card, recent-play history, and exchange counts for that staged step.

Submit

- Commits the staged Human plays.
- If the committed play emptied the hand, the normal empty-hand rules apply.
- Otherwise, the exchange continues or resolves normally.

End Chain

- Ends a live chain and passes or resolves the exchange.
- If unsubmitted Human cards are pending, End Chain is blocked until the Human uses Submit or Undo.

- Full staged play applies only to normal Human attack/defense turns.
- Faceoffs, save chains, rebound saves, and Stabilize attempts use their own one-card selection flow and still require Submit.

16.1 MOMENTUM TIMING WINDOWS

Momentum actions are controlled windows, not always-on abilities.

- Momentum is capped at 3.
- Momentum actions are only available to the current attacker at the start of a new attack.
- They are not available during faceoffs, save chains, defense turns, or Stabilize attempts.
- They are also not available after the attacker has already committed cards into the current attack chain.
- Gain 1 Momentum when you cross into a new major zone band (defensive, neutral, offensive).
- Gain 1 Momentum when you win an exchange by 3 or more cards.
- Gain 1 Momentum when you successfully Stabilize.
- Lose 1 Momentum when you cannot play and must draw.
- Lose all Momentum on any faceoff.
- Lose all Momentum after any shot is created.
- Normal possession loss by itself does not remove Momentum.
- Cycle costs 1 Momentum and replaces one card from hand with a draw.
- Burn costs 2 Momentum and removes one card from hand to the bottom of the deck.
- Force Shot costs 3 Momentum and is only available from a shot zone.
- Ice Puck / Icing only appears when the attacker is in the attack-start window, is stuck in the player's own defensive zone, and has no legal opening play.
- Ice Puck / Icing is a defensive reset: it clears all Momentum, refills both hands, and creates an Icing Faceoff in the icing side's defensive zone.
- Dump Hand only appears when the attacker is on the opponent's side, has not started the attack chain yet, and has no legal opening play.
- Dump Hand is a deliberate field-position reset, not a free attack: it sends the puck deep and refreshes the hand, but it concedes the current exchange and gives up immediate possession.

17. PROTOTYPE OPTIONS

The debug setup can change the ruleset for testing.

Available settings include:

- Suit count: 2 to 5
- Maximum card value: 5 to 10
- Hand size: 3 to 10
- Wild card count: 0 to 20
- CPU speed percentage
- Decks per period: 1 to 5
- Cross River Once: on or off
- Momentum System: on or off

The debug panel also includes a Save Defaults button that stores current debug defaults in browser local storage for future sessions on the same browser/device.

Default prototype configuration

- 4 suits
- Values 1-8
- Hand size 5
- 0 wild cards
- CPU speed 200%
- Decks per period 4
- Cross River Once OFF
- Momentum System ON

18. CURRENT PROTOTYPE LIMITS

These are important when gathering feedback.

- The game ends by period/overtime flow (3 regulation periods, then sudden-death overtime as needed), not by a target score setting.
- The CPU plays automatically on its turn.
- Human faceoffs, Human save chains, Human rebound saves, and Human Stabilize attempts use one-card selection plus Submit rather than the normal staged multi-card flow.
- The rules have been implemented for prototype clarity and testability, not yet for final presentation language.

19. SHORT PLAY SUMMARY

1. Faceoff decides possession.
2. Winner's faceoff card becomes the active card.
3. Attacker starts an exchange by matching the active card.
4. Attacker may continue the chain while legal plays remain.
5. Defender answers with legal matching cards.
6. Compare chain lengths when the exchange ends.
7. If the defender leads by exactly 1, the attacker gets one Stabilize attempt; if the defender leads by 2 or more, the turnover is immediate.
8. Attacker advances only on a true edge in card count; with Momentum ON, 0-0 instead triggers a Loose Puck faceoff in place.
9. A stuck attacker in the defensive zone may choose Icing: clear Momentum, refill both hands, then resolve an Icing Faceoff with the icing side revealing first.
10. If the attacker empties the hand in a shot zone, create a shot sequence. Hands do not refill until the shot sequence ends.
11. Defender must complete the required save chain based on shot value.
12. Full save chain = frozen puck. Exactly 1 short on a 2+ card save requirement = rebound. Otherwise = goal.
13. On a rebound, the shooter reveals the top deck card. Legal reveal = immediate rebound shot. Illegal reveal = defensive recovery.
14. Rebound save needs 1 legal card; if none are in hand, the defender gets 1 desperation reveal from the deck.
15. Rebound save value greater than or equal to the rebound shot value freezes the puck. Lower value creates another rebound.
16. Goal usually resets to center ice (except sudden-death overtime). Frozen puck resets to a local faceoff. Defensive recovery gives possession to the defender in their own goal zone.

20. FEEDBACK QUESTIONS THIS RULESET IS LIKELY TO PROMPT

If this document is being used for feedback, the biggest rules questions are probably:

- Is the faceoff suit cycle intuitive?
- Is the attacker/defender exchange counting easy to read?
- Does the 0-0 Loose Puck faceoff flow (with Momentum ON) feel right?
- Is the defender empty-hand special turnover too strong, too weak, or just right?
- Is the save-chain table easy to learn?
- Is free matching better than single-cross river mode?

End of rulebook.
